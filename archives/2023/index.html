Currently, I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Mathematics, The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) under the supervision of Ronald Lok Ming Lui. I received my Bachelor’s degree in Applied Mathematics from Hunan University in 2020.

My current research enthusiasm lies in Computational Differential Geometry, Medical Imaging, 3D Imaging, Surface Registration Problems and Machine Learning.

I have collaborated with researchers while working at Hunan University and the University of California, Los Angeles.

Featured Works

3D Partial Face Registration
In this work, we mainly focus on 3D partial face meshes and use the quasi-conformal theory at the first step to parameterize the mesh in the least distorted way. Then we developed a learned method to automatically detect the landmarks’ locations on a partial face and determine the existence of each prominent point. Then we generate the Beltrami coefficients and solve the corresponding quasi-conformal mapping to register the partial face based on the detected landmarks and curvature value on each vertex. In this way, bijectivity is preserved by quasi-conformal mapping, and we could easily use this method to compare the difference between partial faces even with few overlapping.

Brain Cortical Surfaces Registration
In medical imaging, surface registration is extensively used for performing systematic comparisons between anatomical structures, with a prime example being the highly convoluted brain cortical surfaces. In this work, we propose a novel framework for the automatic landmark detection and registration of brain cortical surfaces using quasi-conformal geometry and convolutional neural networks.
